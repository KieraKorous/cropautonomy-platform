# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Agricultural operators interested in robotics, research partners, hardware collaborators, robotics engineers, field teams, and early adopters. They arrive with a higher technical baseline than `cropautonomy.com` visitors and a lower tolerance for vague hardware promises. They want to know which platforms exist (GAIA-R, GAIA-D), what stage they are at, and how to engage as a partner, researcher, or early adopter.

## Product Purpose

`gaiabots.ai` is the public landing for GaiaBots, the robotics and device brand within the CropAutonomy ecosystem. It exists to (1) introduce the upcoming GAIA-R (ground rover) and GAIA-D (aerial drone) platforms with technical credibility, (2) signal that captures from GAIA devices feed the same `captures` analysis pipeline as operator Field Capture on CropAutonomy, and (3) capture qualified leads from operators, research partners, and hardware collaborators via the shared `capturePublicLead` flow.

The site also seeds the future home of GaiaBots device knowledge and documentation, but should not yet present a documentation surface. Success is qualified partner and researcher leads, not raw traffic.

## Brand Personality

Precise, technical, exploratory, credible, field-aware. Industrial and agricultural, calm and considered. Hardware development progress should read as evidence of capability, not a manifesto. The voice is closer to mission control telemetry than to consumer hardware launch copy.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS templates (the hero-metric template, identical card grids, gradient accents as identity)
- Playful consumer visuals or soft startup styling
- Neon gaming aesthetics
- Decorative gradients as the main identity
- Fake screenshots or rendered hardware that imply commercial availability
- Editorial / magazine-typographic aesthetics
- Toy-like robot branding
- Sci-fi exaggeration (no chrome, no glow, no "the future of farming" headlines)
- Consumer gadget styling (Apple-keynote product shots, unboxing aesthetic)
- A "GAIA-U" or "GAIA-User" card in the device grid: Field Capture is not a GaiaBots device

## Design Principles

1. **Honest about what ships.** GAIA-R and GAIA-D are upcoming. Roadmap framing, prototype-target dates, no implied commercial availability.
2. **Field-credible, not desk-credible.** Hardware presented in the context of real agricultural operations, not studio product photography.
3. **Tools, not theater.** Restrained motion. Telemetry and technical detail over cinematic hero treatments.
4. **One pipeline, many sources.** Reinforce that GAIA device captures and operator Field Capture feed the same analysis surface. Never position GaiaBots as a parallel product silo.
5. **Precision over polish.** Calm density, technical typography, no marketing flourish. The reader should feel they are looking at an engineering org, not a brand campaign.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. Never indicate state with color alone. Body line length 65 to 75ch. Any device-spec tables must use real semantic table markup with proper headers. Lead capture form must be fully keyboard-navigable with visible focus states and explicit error association.
